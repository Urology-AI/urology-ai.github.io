# Tewari Lab

Welcome to the Tewari Lab at the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai. We are a research lab focused on prostate cancer care, spanning pre-op risk prediction, robotic surgery, and post-op outcomes.

**Meetings:** The lab meets regularly at Mount Sinai to review project progress, discuss new research, and coordinate across the clinical and engineering sides of our work.

Meetings are open to students, trainees, and collaborators. We welcome beginners, practitioners, and researchers alike. Sessions typically cover project updates, clinical-research discussions, short technical tutorials, and coverage of the latest work in surgical AI and prostate cancer research.

## What do we do?

We work on projects that bridge clinical research and engineering, including:

- Risk calculators and clinical decision support tools
- 3D digital twin tools for pre-op surgical planning
- Computer vision on robotic-assisted radical prostatectomy videos
- Vision-language and large language model work for surgical understanding and clinical text
- Clinical cohort construction from Mount Sinai's research databases
- Deployment of clinical tools for surgeons, residents, and patients

## How do we do it?

The lab is organized into three layers that work together:

**The data layer.** Handles clinical data collection and entry, cohort pulls from Mount Sinai's clinical databases, and creation of validation datasets for the lab's risk models. Every study, tool, and manuscript starts with this work.

**The machine learning layer.** Covers computer vision work on robotic prostatectomy videos and vision-language model work for surgical understanding, along with large language model work on clinical text.

**The software and infrastructure layer.** Turns the research into tools that surgeons and patients actually use, such as calculators and clinical tools, along with model validation and the data pipelines, storage, and access layers that tie everything together.

We use machine learning, computer vision, natural language processing, and deep learning across Python, JavaScript, SQL, and modern web stacks, running on Mount Sinai's Minerva HPC cluster with GPU compute.
